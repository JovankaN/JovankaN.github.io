What is the difference between margin, border and padding?

    • What we see in the picture is a Box-Model and it points out various layers of a page element. Lets get down to business and clarify what we actually see in that Box-Model. First we see that our content is first surrounded in a layer called Padding. This falls inside of the element border, and works on all four sides of the inline elements: top, right,bottom and left .Starting from the top then moving clockwise all the way to the left side. There are different ways but one of the common one is the following way:
    padding: 30px 60px 80px 100px
    -top padding is 30px
    -right padding id 60px
    -bottom is 80px
    -left 100px
    Border is the space between the Padding and Margin, in the way that it goes around all four sides of the content is very similar as Padding.
    content{
    border: 1px solid;
    }
    Margin is the space outside the border, the space between the border and the next content.It pretty much allows us to set the amount of space that surrounds the element. The Margin clears an area around an element, and it does not have a color. It works like the Padding starting from the top then clockwise all the way to the left side. One of the way to define it is this:
    - margin : 20px 0px 10px 30px
    -top margin is 20px
    -right margin is 0px
    -bottom is 10px
    -left margin is 30px
